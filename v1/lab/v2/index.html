About

Prof. Dr. D.Sc. Abebe Geletu is the German Research Chair at the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS) Rwanda and a senior academic leader at the AIMS Research & Innovation Center (AIMS–RIC).

His work lies at the intersection of mathematical optimization, autonomous systems, artificial intelligence, and intelligent control, with a strong focus on translating rigorous theory into practical, real‑world impact.

He is particularly interested in developing scalable, data‑driven, and optimization‑based methodologies for complex cyber‑physical systems, energy systems, and sustainable infrastructures, with relevance to both African and global challenges.

Education

  • B.Sc. Mathematics – Addis Ababa University
  • M.Sc. Applied Mathematics – Addis Ababa University
  • Ph.D. Numerical Optimization – TU Ilmenau, Germany
  • D.Sc. (Habilitation) Systems Optimization – TU Ilmenau, Germany

Research Agenda

My research agenda aims to advance the theory and practice of optimization‑driven intelligent systems that operate reliably under uncertainty, limited resources, and real‑world constraints.

Core Themes

  • Optimization methods for large‑scale, nonlinear, and stochastic systems
  • Model Predictive Control (MPC) for autonomous and cyber‑physical systems
  • Hybrid AI models combining learning and optimization
  • Data‑driven decision‑making for sustainable energy and infrastructure systems

Methodological Focus

The work integrates numerical optimization, control theory, machine learning, and system identification, with emphasis on interpretability, robustness, and deployability.

Impact & Vision

A central goal is to contribute tools and methodologies that support sustainable development, resilient infrastructure, and intelligent automation, particularly in emerging and resource‑constrained environments.
