About the Lab & Articles

The Autonomous Systems Lab at AIMS–RIC focuses on hybrid AI, autonomous Sytems (AV and UAV), cyber-physical security, and sustainable energy systems. We combine rigorous mathematical foundations with practical implementations to solve real-world challenges.

Recent articles highlight our work on intelligent predictive control, distributed energy optimization, and AI-based intrusion detection. We aim to empower the next generation of engineers and researchers through collaboration, innovation, and ethical AI practices.

Prof. Dr. D.Sc. Abebe Geletu is the German Research Chair at the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS) Rwanda and a senior academic leader at the AIMS Research & Innovation Center (AIMS–RIC). His work lies at the intersection of mathematical optimization, autonomous systems, artificial intelligence, and intelligent control, with a strong focus on translating rigorous theory into practical, real‑world impact. He is particularly interested in developing scalable, data‑driven, and optimization‑based methodologies for complex cyber‑physical systems, energy systems, and sustainable infrastructures, with relevance to both African and global challenges.
